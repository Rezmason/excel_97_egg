Artists and craftspeople often like to leave their mark on what they make. Computer programmers are no different.

The basic flight simulator you see here is an example of an "easter egg" — a game, image or message hidden in a creative work, which in this case is Microsoft Excel 97.

Users who performed a secret sequence of unlikely actions in an empty spreadsheet whisked away to this lavender lunar landscape, created by members of its "Charting Team", beside a scrolling stele glorifying the program and listing everyone who worked on it.

This HTML port is meant to be a faithful reproduction of the actual easter egg. At first, its frame rate and drawing resolution are artificially constrained to the conventional limitations in 1997, but this can be toggled in the toolbar, along with additional visual embellishments.

This project is open source and MIT licensed.
